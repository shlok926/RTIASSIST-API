## Tool 1 — Second Appeal (CIC)

### What is it?
Jab aapka RTI application ignore ho — PIO ne reply nahi diya ya incomplete/wrong info di — aur First Appeal bhi fail ho gayi, tab **Central Information Commission (CIC)** mein Second Appeal file hoti hai.

### When to Use
- PIO ne 30 din mein reply nahi diya
- Reply incomplete / incorrect / misleading thi
- Bina valid reason ke information deny ki gayi
- First Appeal ka response unsatisfactory tha

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1 — Open the Tool**
> `⚖️ Legal Tools` (navbar) → `📝 Second Appeal (CIC)`

**Step 2 — Fill the Form**

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| Your Full Name | Your legal name | Rahul Sharma |
| RTI Application Date | Date when you filed RTI | 15/01/2026 |
| First Appeal Date | Date when you filed First Appeal | 20/02/2026 |
| Department Name | Department where RTI was filed | PWD, Maharashtra |
| RTI Query | Exact question you had asked | Status of road repair work in Ward 5 |
| Reason for Appeal | Select from dropdown | No Response / Incomplete / Denied |
| Additional Details | Optional extra context | (leave blank or add 1-2 lines) |

**Step 3 — Generate**
> Click `Generate Second Appeal` → Document ready in seconds

**Step 4 — What You Get**
- Formally drafted Second Appeal letter addressed to **CIC Registrar, New Delhi**
- Cites RTI Act Sections: 7(1), 19(1), 19(3), 20(1)
- Includes grounds, relief sought (info + penalty + compensation)
- Enclosures checklist included

**Step 5 — How to File**
1. Copy/Download the generated document
2. Send via **Registered Post with AD** to:
   > Central Information Commission, August Kranti Bhawan, Bhikaji Cama Place, New Delhi - 110066
3. OR file online at: **cic.gov.in → File Complaint**
4. No filing fee required

### Key Law
> **RTI Act 2005 — Section 19(3):** "Any person who does not receive a decision within the time specified... or is aggrieved by a decision of the First Appellate Authority... may within ninety days from the expiry of such period... prefer a second appeal to the Central Information Commission."

## Tool 2 — Consumer Complaint

### What is it?
Kisi company/seller ne defective product diya, service nahi di, refund refuse kiya, ya misleading ad se cheat kiya — tab **District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission** mein complaint file hoti hai.

### When to Use
- Product defective nikla, replacement/refund nahi mila
- Service provider ne kaam poora nahi kiya
- Builder ne possession delay kiya (RERA + Consumer Court)
- Insurance claim reject hua bina valid reason ke
- Bank / hospital / telecom company ne loss pahuchaya

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1 — Open the Tool**
> `⚖️ Legal Tools` → `🛒 Consumer Complaint`

**Step 2 — Fill the Form**

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| Your Full Name | Your legal name | Priya Mehta |
| Your Address | Full postal address | 12, MG Road, Pune - 411001 |
| Company/Seller Name | Exact business name | Flipkart Internet Pvt. Ltd. |
| Complaint Type | Select from dropdown | Defective Product / Refund Denied / Builder Delay |
| Problem Description | What happened, when, how | Ordered laptop on 10 Jan, received broken screen, 3 emails ignored |
| Amount Involved | Exact rupee amount | 45000 |
| Purchase Date | Date of transaction | 10/01/2026 |
| Relief Sought | What you want | Refund / Replacement / Compensation |

**Step 3 — Generate**
> Click `Generate Complaint` → Formal complaint draft ready

**Step 4 — What You Get**
- Full court-formatted complaint for District Consumer Commission
- Cites Consumer Protection Act 2019 — Section 2(7), 2(11), 2(47), 35
- Includes parties, facts, legal grounds, all reliefs (refund + compensation + litigation cost)
- Ready-to-print format

**Step 5 — How to File**
1. Print the document on plain paper
2. Make 3 copies (original + 2)
3. Attach proofs: bill, screenshots, emails, photos
4. File at **District Consumer Commission** of your district
5. Filing fee: ₹0 for claims up to ₹5 lakh (online filing free at **edaakhil.nic.in**)

### Key Law
> **Consumer Protection Act, 2019 — Section 35:** A consumer may file a complaint with the District Commission for deficiency in service or unfair trade practice involving amount up to **₹1 crore**.

## Tool 3 — Legal Notice

### What is it?
Court mein jaane se pehle opponent ko ek formal warning letter bhejna padta hai — usse kehte hain **Legal Notice**. Yeh ek last chance hota hai ki woh apni galti sudhare. Aksar is notice se hi kaam ho jaata hai court ke bina.

### When to Use
- Kisi ne paise nahi laute (loan, deposit, advance)
- Cheque bounce hua
- Builder ne flat nahi diya / delay kiya
- Employer ne salary/gratuity/PF nahi diya
- Kisi ne contract tora
- Property/rent dispute

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1 — Open the Tool**
> `⚖️ Legal Tools` → `📜 Legal Notice`

**Step 2 — Fill the Form**

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| Your Name (Sender) | Your full legal name | Amit Kumar |
| Recipient Name | Person/company to send notice to | M/s ABC Builders Pvt. Ltd. |
| Notice Type | Select from dropdown | Builder Delay / Cheque Bounce / Money Recovery |
| Problem Description | Clear description of issue | Builder promised possession by Dec 2024, not given yet. ₹40 lakh paid |
| Amount | Rupee amount at stake | 400000 |
| Additional Details | Optional | (any other relevant facts) |

**Step 3 — Generate**
> Click `Generate Legal Notice` → Notice ready

**Step 4 — What You Get**
- Formal legal notice mentioning exact law sections per issue type:
  - Cheque Bounce → Section 138/139/141 Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881
  - Builder Delay → RERA Act 2016 Section 18 + Consumer Protection Act 2019
  - Money Recovery → Order 37 CPC
  - Employment → Payment of Wages Act / Industrial Disputes Act
  - Rent/Property → Transfer of Property Act, 1882
- 15-day compliance demand
- Legal consequences of non-compliance clearly stated

**Step 5 — How to Send**
1. Send via **Registered Post with Acknowledgement Due (RPAD)**
2. Keep the postal receipt + delivery acknowledgement safely
3. If recipient ignores within 15 days → file case in appropriate court/forum
4. Optional: Get it countersigned by a local advocate for stronger impact

### Key Law
> Legal notices are governed by **Section 80 CPC** (for govt), **Section 138 NI Act** (cheque bounce — mandatory before filing case), **RERA Section 18** (builder delay). Notice is NOT legally mandatory in all cases but strongly recommended before litigation.

## Tool 4 — Labour Complaint

### What is it?
Employer ne salary nahi di, wrongfully terminate kiya, PF nahi bhara, ya koi labour law violation hua — tab **Labour Commissioner Office** mein formal complaint ki jaati hai.

### When to Use
- Employer ne ek ya zyada months ki salary nahi di
- Bina notice ke / illegally terminate kiya
- PF (Provident Fund) deduct hua par EPFO mein deposit nahi hua
- ESIC (health insurance) benefit nahi diya
- Overtime ka paisa nahi mila
- Resignation ke baad gratuity / full & final settlement nahi mila

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1 — Open the Tool**
> `⚖️ Legal Tools` → `👷 Labour Complaint`

**Step 2 — Fill the Form**

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| Your Full Name | Your legal name | Suresh Patel |
| Employer / Company Name | Exact company name | XYZ Manufacturing Pvt. Ltd. |
| Your State | State where you worked | Gujarat |
| Complaint Type | Select from dropdown | Salary Unpaid / PF Not Deposited / Wrongful Termination |
| Problem Description | Detailed explanation | Worked from Jan 2025–Dec 2025. Last 3 months salary (₹36,000) not paid despite multiple requests |
| Amount | Rupee amount owed | 36000 |

**Step 3 — Generate**
> Click `Generate Labour Complaint` → Official complaint letter ready

**Step 4 — What You Get**
- Formal complaint letter addressed to **Labour Commissioner, [Your State]**
- Cites exact law sections per complaint type:
  - Salary Unpaid → Section 15, Payment of Wages Act, 1936
  - Wrongful Termination → Section 25F, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947
  - PF Not Deposited → Section 14A/14B, EPF & Misc Provisions Act, 1952
  - ESIC Not Provided → Section 85, ESI Act, 1948
  - Overtime Unpaid → Section 59, Factories Act, 1948
  - Gratuity Denied → Section 4, Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972
- Relief demands: payment + penalty on employer
- Enclosures checklist

**Step 5 — How to File**
1. Print the letter and take it to the **District Labour Office** of your area
2. OR file online via your state's Labour Department portal
3. For PF-specific complaints → also file at: **epfindia.gov.in → Grievance**
4. For ESIC complaints → file at: **esic.in → Grievance Portal**
5. Attach: Appointment Letter, Salary Slips, Bank Statements, ID proof

### Key Law
> **Payment of Wages Act, 1936 — Section 15:** Application for claims to the Authority shall be made within one year of the date on which the deduction was made or delay in payment occurred.

## API Reference

All 4 tools share a single endpoint.

### `POST /legal/generate`

**Request Body:**
```json
{
  "tool": "second_appeal | consumer_complaint | legal_notice | labour_complaint",
  "name": "Applicant Name",
  "department": "Dept Name (for second_appeal)",
  "query": "RTI query text (for second_appeal)",
  "rti_date": "DD/MM/YYYY",
  "appeal_date": "DD/MM/YYYY",
  "reason": "no_response | incomplete | denied | wrong_info | partial | delay",
  "company": "Company name (for consumer_complaint)",
  "address": "Your address",
  "type": "defective_product | service_deficiency | refund_denied | ...",
  "description": "Detailed problem description",
  "amount": "50000",
  "purchase_date": "DD/MM/YYYY",
  "relief": "refund | replacement | compensation | all",
  "sender": "Sender name (for legal_notice)",
  "recipient": "Recipient name (for legal_notice)",
  "employer": "Employer name (for labour_complaint)",
  "state": "State name (for labour_complaint)",
  "details": "Optional additional details"
}
```

**Response:**
```json
{
  "draft": "...(full generated legal document text)...",
  "tool": "second_appeal",
  "status": "success"
}
```

**Required Fields per Tool:**

| Tool | Required Fields |
|------|----------------|
| `second_appeal` | `name`, `department`, `query` |
| `consumer_complaint` | `name`, `company`, `description` |
| `legal_notice` | `sender`, `recipient`, `description` |
| `labour_complaint` | `name`, `employer`, `description` |
